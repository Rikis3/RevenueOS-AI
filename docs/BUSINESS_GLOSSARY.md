# Business Glossary

This glossary standardizes the terminology used by the data team, marketing, sales, and executive leadership to ensure alignment across RevenueOS.

| Term | Definition | Synonyms / Related Terms |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Account** | A company or organization that is a prospective or current customer. B2B sales are account-centric. | Company, Organization, Logo |
| **Contact** | An individual person associated with an Account. Often converted from a Lead. | Person, Stakeholder |
| **Lead** | An individual person expressing interest, but not yet qualified or associated with a formal Account/Opportunity structure. | Prospect |
| **Opportunity** | A qualified potential sale. It tracks the deal's dollar value, expected close date, and current stage in the sales process. | Deal, Opp |
| **Pipeline** | The cumulative value of all open (active) Opportunities. | Open Pipeline, Open Deals |
| **ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)** | The annualized value of all subscription contracts. A $10k/month contract equals $120k ARR. | - |
| **Attribution** | The mathematical process of assigning credit for a Closed-Won opportunity back to the marketing touchpoints that influenced it. | Campaign Influence |
| **Funnel Leakage** | The points in the sales process where prospective customers drop out or are disqualified. | Drop-off Rate |
| **Medallion Architecture** | A data design pattern: Bronze (Raw), Silver (Cleaned/Conformed), Gold (Business/Aggregated). | Data Warehouse Layers |
| **SDR (Sales Development Rep)** | A junior sales role focused entirely on prospecting and qualifying Leads into SQLs for Account Executives. | BDR, Inside Sales |
| **AE (Account Executive)** | A senior sales role focused on closing SQLs. They manage the opportunity from Discovery to Closed Won. | Closer, Sales Rep |
| **Closed Won** | The final stage of an opportunity indicating a signed contract and realized revenue. | Won Deal |
| **Closed Lost** | The final stage of an opportunity indicating the prospect chose a competitor or decided not to buy. | Lost Deal |
| **Campaign** | A specific marketing initiative (e.g., "Q3 Webinar", "LinkedIn Retargeting") designed to generate leads and pipeline. | Marketing Initiative |
